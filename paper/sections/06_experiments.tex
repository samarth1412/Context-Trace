\section{Experiments}
We ask whether ContextTrace detects failures (RQ1), localizes diagnostic
structure (RQ2), depends on each pipeline stage (RQ3), and improves repair
decisions (RQ4). The full command evaluates the frozen Diag-150 and RAGTruth
inputs, scores the same-ID RAGAS prediction file, runs all executable ablations,
and regenerates tables and manifests. Quick runs are harness checks and are
never paper results.

Table~\ref{tab:table1-main-results} reports the pre-review external results.
ContextTrace obtains 0.955 failure-label macro-F1, 0.955 root-cause accuracy,
1.000 citation-error F1, 0.786 span overlap, and zero dangerous false greens on
the 200 RAGTruth cases. Diag-150 scores are 1.000 for failure, root-cause, and
citation metrics, with 0.950 span overlap. These estimates are calibration
evidence because independent review is incomplete.

Table~\ref{tab:table2-baselines} compares common outputs on identical RAGTruth
IDs. ContextTrace has higher failure-label F1 and lower false-green rate than the
cached RAGAS row, while RAGAS has higher claim-verdict F1 (0.533 versus 0.337).
RAGAS does not expose matched root-cause, citation, or span fields in this cache;
we report N/A rather than attributing implicit zeros. Consequently, the results
do not establish broad dominance.
