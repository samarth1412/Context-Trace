\section{Ethics Statement}
ContextTrace can process traces locally, reducing the need to transmit prompts,
answers, or retrieved documents to an external evaluator. Local execution does
not remove obligations around access control, retention, dataset licenses, or
personal information contained in traces. Users should minimize stored content,
restrict report access, and avoid publishing sensitive evidence spans.

Automatic diagnoses can be wrong and should not be the sole basis for decisions
in medical, legal, financial, employment, or other high-impact settings. A
``supported'' result means supported by supplied evidence, not true in the
world. Repair suggestions require engineering validation and a recaptured
passing trace.

The simulated-review pilot is explicitly labeled to prevent model outputs from
being misrepresented as human judgments. Future human participation must be
voluntary, appropriately reviewed where required, compensated consistently,
and reported without identifying reviewers in the anonymous submission. Raw
responses and disagreements should be retained rather than selectively removed.
