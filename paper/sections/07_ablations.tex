\section{Ablations}
Table~\ref{tab:table3-ablations} reports eleven registered variants on 500
engineering cases. Nine execute through production profile switches. Lexical-
and semantic-only verification each obtain 0.419 failure F1 and a 0.232 false-
green rate, showing that claim matching alone is insufficient for trace-level
diagnosis. Removing citation alignment lowers failure F1 to 0.831 and raises
false greens to 0.122. Removing source trust/freshness produces the largest
available full-pipeline degradation: 0.708 failure F1 and 0.112 false greens.

Contradiction checks primarily affect claim decisions: without them,
claim-verdict F1 falls to 0.706 and failure F1 to 0.812. Removing abstention
logic leaves citation and claim metrics intact but lowers root-cause accuracy to
0.372. Removing the root-cause classifier leaves detection unchanged while
correctly making root-cause accuracy unavailable. Removing evidence-span
localization makes span overlap unavailable and slightly changes claim and
failure behavior because localized evidence also constrains downstream matching.

NLI-only and judge-only rows are N/A. Downloading a model after viewing results
would violate the frozen contract; pinning these variants is future work rather
than a reason to omit their registered rows.
